Socieconomic & Behavioral Risk Factors for Healthcare

Insights Discovered Looking at 2014 Between Health Risks and Age, Income

Analysis: Percentage of population In Poverty. The United States has among the largest income-based health disparities in the world. THis analysis confirms this. As the poverty rate increases, there is an increase in all health concerns. A closer look reveals that this trend is magnified in Southeastern states: higher poverty rates accompanied by higher incidences of poor health.

Analysis: Age (Median). The age effect was mixed. There didn't seem to be a direct corelation between age and health outcomes across the board. The findings indicate that there are other factors beyond age influencing health outcomes. Socioeconomic and behavioral factors seem to have a stronger influence on health ouotcomes than age related effects. Consider West virginia which has a higher age median, better healthcare, but higher percentages of obesity and smokers. Also Northeastern States as having a higher age median and better healthcare.

Analysis: Household Income( Median). On the surface there seems to be a direct relationship between household income and the occurrence of obesity and smoking. I would submit that the relationship is indirect. High poverty rates would skew median income to the left creating the phenomenon we are seeing. Lower median income is tied to higher poverty rates which is tied to higher incidence of obesity and adverse health behaviors (smoking). The data shows that poorer regions of the country tend to have more adverse health conditions The Southeastern States rank poorer than other states for income and healthcare, and the Northeastern states are faring better.
